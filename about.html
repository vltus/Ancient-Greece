🏛️ Ancient Greece
About Episodes

The Wonders of Ancient Greece is an in-depth interactive experience in the form of immersive episodes developed in consideration for all demographics. It is aimed at working as an alternative educational aid by adopting video game graphics and a slider to control the episodes time.

Each episode recreates a moment of the ancient Greek world in real-time 3D — from the roar of the chariot arena to the marble heights of the Acropolis and the legendary gates of Troy. Rather than watching a fixed documentary, viewers take control: the timeline slider lets you scrub backwards and forwards through each scene at your own pace, pausing on the details that interest you and revisiting moments as often as you like.

The project was built with accessibility in mind. By using familiar video game visuals instead of dense text or static imagery, it aims to make ancient history approachable for younger learners, engaging for students, and enjoyable for anyone with a curiosity about the classical world. It is designed to sit alongside traditional teaching materials as an alternative, hands-on way of exploring the past.

The series currently includes three complete episodes — The Chariot Races, A Tour Around Acropolis and The Trojan Horse — with more scenes of the ancient world in development. Head over to the Episodes page to watch them.
